The nice thing about containers is that they help us think about 1 thing at a time. But what we are seeing here with floats are things outside of containers jumping in. The workflow of dealing with overall layout first (the artist drawing big circles), and then dealing with each individual container, is great. Its a good separation of concerns. If things outside any given container can make their way in, then this whole way of working breaks down. In my code, I only want things inside my container (in html) to be in my container. I dont want floats crossing that boundary and making their way in. So how do we solve this?

A safer solution is to give floats their own container and have them only interact with other floats, dont let them interact with anyone else. That way things are more predictable.

But of course this means they are no longer taking up any space, so we can give the container an absolute height:

But absolute heights are dangerous, its safer to let the content define the height, so instead we give it a clear fix.

As long as floats are in their own container, they can not jump out and get into other containers
